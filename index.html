Motion in 1D (Rectilinear)

The 3 Equations (Constant Acceleration only)

Valid only when $\vec{a} = \text{constant}$.

$$v = u + at$$ $$s = ut + \frac{1}{2}at^2$$ $$v^2 = u^2 + 2as$$

Remember to apply sign convention! (Usually Up/Right = Positive).

Calculus Methods JEE ADVANCED

When acceleration depends on time ($t$), displacement ($x$), or velocity ($v$).

1. Acceleration as f(t): $$v = \int a \cdot dt$$

2. Acceleration as f(x): (Most important trick)

$$a = \frac{dv}{dt} = \frac{dv}{dx} \cdot \frac{dx}{dt} = v \frac{dv}{dx}$$ $$\int v \cdot dv = \int a(x) \cdot dx$$

Motion in 2D (Projectile)

Independent Axes Principle

X-axis motion and Y-axis motion are independent of each other.

  • X-axis: $a_x = 0$ (Uniform velocity)
  • Y-axis: $a_y = -g$ (Uniform acceleration)

Trajectory Equation

Eliminating 't' gives the path of the particle:

$$y = x\tan\theta - \frac{gx^2}{2u^2\cos^2\theta}$$

This represents a Parabola.

Relative Motion

General Definition

Velocity of A with respect to B:

$$\vec{v}_{AB} = \vec{v}_A - \vec{v}_B$$

Acceleration of A with respect to B:

$$\vec{a}_{AB} = \vec{a}_A - \vec{a}_B$$

River-Boat Problems JEE CLASSIC

Let $\vec{v}_{br}$ be velocity of boat relative to river (engine speed).

Let $\vec{v}_{r}$ be velocity of river flow.

Actual Velocity: $$\vec{v}_b = \vec{v}_{br} + \vec{v}_r$$

Minimum Crossing Time: Head perpendicular to flow ($t = w / v_{br}$).

Shortest Path (Drift = 0): Head upstream at angle $\sin\theta = v_r / v_{br}$.
